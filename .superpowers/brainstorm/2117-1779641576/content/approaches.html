Three approaches to the store listing

Same product, different angle. Which framing fits how your target user thinks about their problem?

A

"Never copy-paste again" — friction-removal first

Lead with the pain: job seekers spend 20 minutes per application just moving text around. JobSearch Aide eliminates that. Google Sheets and the AI-ready file are features that support the headline, not the headline itself. Best for users who don't yet know they want this tool — impulse discovery in the store.

Pros

  • Broad appeal
  • Pain is universally felt
  • Low explanation needed

Cons

  • Doesn't differentiate from generic "job tracker" tools
B

"AI-ready job search" — outcome first (recommended)

Lead with the result: your job search data is clean, structured, and ready for AI tools. The copy-paste friction is the mechanism; AI-readiness is the promise. Speaks directly to someone already using ChatGPT/Claude to tweak resumes who hasn't connected the dots that their workflow could be tighter. Differentiates from every other job tracker.

Pros

  • Distinctive positioning
  • Speaks to exactly your target user
  • Future-proof framing

Cons

  • Requires user to already think in terms of "feeding AI"
C

"Your job search, in your own Google Sheet" — ownership first

Lead with control and privacy: no new app, no subscription, your data stays yours in a spreadsheet you already own. AI-readiness and friction removal are supporting points. Speaks to the user who's skeptical of job-tracker SaaS products and already lives in Sheets.

Pros

  • Strong differentiator from SaaS trackers
  • Trust-building

Cons

  • Narrows audience to Sheets users
  • Buries the AI angle
