Screenshot plan — 5 slides

Order matters: first is shown before clicking "See more." React to sequence and what each should emphasize.

Screenshot 1 — Hero (most important)
Scene: Extension popup open on a real job listing page, Google Sheet visible behind it.
Caption: "One click. Job saved to your spreadsheet."
Goal: Immediately shows the core action and the result in one frame.
Screenshot 2 — The spreadsheet result
Scene: Google Sheet with 4-5 rows of job entries — company, role, link, status, date. Clean, realistic data.
Caption: "Your full job tracker, in a spreadsheet you own."
Goal: Shows the output. Reassures the user their data is organized and theirs.
Screenshot 3 — AI-ready file
Scene: Split view — downloaded job description file on the left, ChatGPT or Claude open on the right with it pasted in.
Caption: "Job description saved and ready to paste into any AI tool."
Goal: Makes the AI-ready angle concrete and visual. Shows the actual workflow payoff.
Screenshot 4 — Outreach on LinkedIn
Scene: Extension popup open on a LinkedIn profile page. Form visible with name pre-filled, role/company fields.
Caption: "Log a contact without leaving LinkedIn."
Goal: Shows the outreach feature exists. Keeps expectations realistic — it's a form, not magic scraping.
Screenshot 5 — Outreach sheet with follow-up dates
Scene: Outreach tab of the Google Sheet — contact name, company, role, date contacted, follow-up date auto-filled.
Caption: "Follow-up dates filled automatically. Know exactly who to reach out to next."
Goal: Shows the payoff of the outreach feature — organized, actionable, no manual date math.
