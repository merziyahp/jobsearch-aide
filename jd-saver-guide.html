JD Saver

Chrome Extension · Free · No account required

Stop copying and pasting job descriptions into spreadsheets. JD Saver captures any job posting as a clean markdown file and autofills your standard application fields — all in one click.

💾

Save Job Descriptions as Markdown

One click captures the full job description from any page — Greenhouse, Lever, LinkedIn, Indeed, Workday, or a company's own careers site. Saves as a clean .txt file named Company-Role-Date, stripped of ads and navigation clutter. Works great with Obsidian or any markdown-aware tool.

✍️

Autofill Job Applications

Enter your profile once in Settings — name, email, phone, address, LinkedIn, work authorization. On any application form, click Fill Form and your standard fields are pre-filled and highlighted green for review. Sensitive EEO fields (disability, veteran status) are flagged in yellow but never auto-filled — those are always your call.

🔍

Works Everywhere

Automatically detects company and role name from the page. Handles React and Vue-based forms correctly. Remembers your save folder between sessions. No login, no account, no subscription.

How it works

1
Open any job posting and click the JD Saver icon in your toolbar.
2
Company and role are auto-detected. Edit if needed, then hit Save as Markdown. File downloads instantly.
3
When you hit Apply, switch to the Autofill tab and click Fill Form. Review the highlighted fields, then submit.
4
Your profile is set once in Settings and remembered — no re-entering your details for every application.
🔒
Your data never leaves your device. Your profile (name, email, address, etc.) is stored locally in Chrome's storage — the same place browser preferences are kept. It is never transmitted to any server, never shared, and is only used to fill forms on your behalf when you explicitly click Fill Form.

Install

  1. Unzip the jd-saver folder
  2. Open Chrome and go to chrome://extensions
  3. Enable Developer mode (toggle, top right)
  4. Click Load unpacked and select the jd-saver folder
  5. Pin the 📄 icon to your toolbar and you're ready
